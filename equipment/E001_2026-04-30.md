## Programme Phases

### Phase 1 — RFI Characterisation (immediate, pre-dish)
RTL-SDR Blog V4 + dipole antenna on Amazon Basics tripod connected
directly to MacBook Pro. Purpose: RFI survey and characterisation of
the Blue Rock site at 1420 MHz before dish arrives. Software familiarisation.
This phase begins as soon as the V4 and dipole arrive.

**Rationale:** The dish arrives in July 2026. Rather than waiting, the
RTL-SDR V4 with dipole provides an immediate wideband RFI survey capability.
A nearly omnidirectional dipole shows the full RFI environment without
directional filtering — ideal for baseline characterisation. Weeks of RFI
survey data will be accumulated before the dish is assembled, providing
a pre-dish baseline that documents the site environment independently
of the HI observing system.

### Phase 2 — HI Commissioning (on dish arrival, July 2026)
RTL-SDR Blog V4 connected to Discovery Dish + HI Feed via 6m LMR200.
Raspberry Pi 3 for dish control and data logging. EZRa for HI data
acquisition. First light, Cas A calibration, initial HI observations.

**SDR upgrade decision:** The V4 will be evaluated against the San Jose
RFI environment with the full dish and SAW filter chain. If ADC saturation
or dynamic range proves limiting, upgrade to Airspy R2 (~$169). If V4
performance is adequate, retain and redirect budget elsewhere.
This is a data-driven decision, not a speculative one.

### Phase 3 — Pipeline Development (Year 2, 2027)
GNU Radio custom pipeline development. Airspy R2 if upgrade warranted.
Full automated observing capability via rotctl.
