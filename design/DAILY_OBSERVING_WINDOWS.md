## Practical Session Planning

### The universal session anchor

**Cas A is always available.** Every session starts and ends with
Cas A regardless of month, time of day, or primary target. No
planning needed — just confirm it's above 30° (it always is)
and begin.

### M31 season sessions (Sep–Jan) — recommended structure

In these months M31, M33, and M1 all have evening windows that
overlap naturally:

```
~20:00  Cas A — opening calibration (15 min)
~20:15  M31 — primary integration (2–3 hrs)
~23:00  M33 — secondary integration (30–60 min) if well placed
~23:30  M1 — flux check (30 min)
~00:00  Cas A — closing calibration (15 min)
```

Adjust start time monthly using the transit table above.

### Off-season sessions — opportunistic approach

Outside M31 season, targets are available but at less convenient
times. Rather than forcing a schedule, observe when the window
aligns naturally with your day:

- M31 in April transiting at 11:42 — observe 09:00–14:00 on a
  calm spring morning
- M31 in July transiting at 05:42 — observe 00:00–09:00 if awake
  (rare but valid)
- Complex C year-round — always available as a fill-in target

### Solar separation reminder

Before every session, check solar separation in Stellarium.
The Sun moves ~1° per day. In summer months when targets transit
during the day, solar separation is the primary scheduling
constraint — not elevation.

## Notes on Timing Accuracy

Transit times are approximate — calculated from mean sidereal
time and will vary by ±15 minutes due to:
- Equation of time (difference between mean and apparent solar time)
- Refraction near horizon
- Exact date within the month

For precise session planning use Stellarium with your exact date
and location. The tables above are for monthly planning — use
Stellarium for session-level precision.

All times are **local time (PST/PDT)** — remember to account for
daylight saving time (PDT = PST + 1 hour, approximately Mar–Nov).
