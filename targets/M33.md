## Comparison with M31

| Parameter | M31 | M33 |
|---|---|---|
| Distance | 785 kpc | 840 kpc |
| HI flux | ~54 Jy km/s | ~15 Jy km/s |
| Systemic velocity | -300 km/s | -179 km/s |
| Angular HI size | ~3° × 1° | ~1° × 0.7° |
| Inclination | ~77° | ~54° |
| Beam filling | Fully unresolved | Fully unresolved |

M33 is approximately 3.5× fainter than M31 in HI flux. Its lower
inclination means the double-horn profile is less pronounced — at 54°
inclination the two horns are closer together and the profile is
more triangular in shape. This makes M33 a more challenging detection
but a useful comparison object in the same observing season.

---

## Detection Feasibility

With Blue Rock SEFD ~138,000 Jy and M33 HI flux ~15 Jy km/s:

The integrated flux is spread across ~120 km/s velocity width.
At 1420 MHz, 120 km/s corresponds to ~570 kHz bandwidth.
Peak flux per channel at 1 km/s resolution: ~15/120 ≈ 0.125 Jy/channel.

Detection requires substantially more integration than M31.
Estimated integration for SNR = 5 on integrated profile:
- Bandwidth: ~570 kHz (velocity width)
- Required: several hours per session, many sessions co-added
- Feasible over the four-year programme — not a single-session target

M33 is a multi-season co-addition target. Individual sessions contribute
to the cumulative dataset even if not individually significant.

## Expected Spectral Profile

At 54° inclination M33 shows a double-horn profile but less pronounced
than M31. The two horns are closer in velocity separation and the
central dip is shallower. The profile is broader relative to the
peak amplitude than for a more edge-on galaxy.

Systemic velocity of -179 km/s means the HI line is blueshifted to
approximately:

$$f_{obs} = 1420.405 \times \left(1 + \frac{179}{299792}\right) \approx 1421.25 \text{ MHz}$$

The full velocity range (-239 to -119 km/s LSR) spans approximately
570 kHz centred near 1421.25 MHz — well within the HI feed passband
and clear of the LO DC spike at 1422 MHz offset.

---

## Role in Blue Rock Programme

M33 serves as a secondary extragalactic HI target in the autumn/winter
season, observed opportunistically when M31 has been well covered in
recent sessions. It provides:

- A second Local Group HI detection demonstrating pipeline generality
- A comparison target at different inclination from M31
- An independent validation of flux calibration via comparison with
  published HI flux measurements
- A more challenging detection that tests the sensitivity limits of
  the system after co-addition

## Notes

M33 is a face-on galaxy by comparison with M31 — at 54° inclination
it is far from edge-on. The HI profile is correspondingly less
double-horned and more complex. Do not expect the clean separated
double-horn morphology seen in M31.

The systemic velocity of -179 km/s means M33 is approaching at about
60% of M31's recession velocity. Both are blueshifted and both sit
comfortably within the HI feed passband at LO = 1422 MHz.
