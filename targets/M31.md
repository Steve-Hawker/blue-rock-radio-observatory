## Beam filling

At 1420 MHz, 70cm dish beamwidth ~17°. M31 HI extent ~3°.
**M31 is unresolved — entire HI flux enters the beam.**
This is advantageous: the integrated flux figure above applies directly.

## Expected spectral profile

Double-horned profile characteristic of an inclined rotating disk.
Velocity range approximately -570 to -30 km/s (LSR).
Peak flux per channel depends on spectral resolution — at 1 km/s resolution,
peak antenna temperature will be well below system temperature; requires integration.

## Professional survey data for comparison

- **HI4PI survey** (Ben Bekhti et al. 2016, A&A 594 A116) — all-sky HI survey,
  resolution 16.2 arcmin, rms ~43 mK. Primary comparison dataset.
- **WSRT observations** — high resolution HI imaging of M31 disk.
- **Chemin et al. 2009** — HI kinematics and rotation curve reference.

## Science goals

## Notes

M31 is approaching (blueshifted), so the HI line appears at ~1420.405 + 1.4 MHz
relative shift — observe at ~1422 MHz or ensure your passband covers the full
velocity range -600 to 0 km/s relative to the HI rest frequency.

Doppler correction is essential and must account for:
- Earth rotation (~0.5 km/s)
- Earth orbital velocity (~30 km/s, seasonal)  
- Solar motion to LSR (~20 km/s)
Use Astropy radial_velocity_correction() for all corrections.
